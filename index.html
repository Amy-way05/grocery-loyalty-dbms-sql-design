What This Reveals

  • The loyalty base is meaningful but leaves a large enrollment opportunity across 1,000 customer records.
  • Promotions average 7.2 per store, making campaign strategy a mature reporting area.
  • Rewards average 9.3 options per tier, supporting tier-based benefit differentiation.
  • Review volume is low relative to customer count, so feedback capture should become an operating priority.

Loyalty participation and tier behavior

Measures loyalty penetration, tier distribution, average points, and high-value customer segments.

SELECT tier_level, COUNT(*) AS accounts, AVG(loyalty_points) AS avg_points
FROM loyalty JOIN tier USING (tier_id)
GROUP BY tier_level
ORDER BY accounts DESC;

Decision Pathways

The outcome is not just a schema; it is a set of decisions that the database makes possible.

Grow Loyalty

Use the 21.2% account ratio as a baseline for enrollment campaigns and tier migration targets.

Optimize Campaigns

Compare store promotion volume with review activity to identify campaigns that create engagement.

Refine Rewards

Evaluate reward depth and cost by tier to avoid underpowered or overly generous benefit structures.

Increase Feedback

Use review participation as a signal for post-purchase feedback workflow improvement.
